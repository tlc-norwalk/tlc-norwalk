What To Expect

Sunday Service

On any given Sunday, our church members arrive about 15 minutes early to eat donuts, drink coffee, and catch up on the week. We usually wear casual clothes to church, so don't feel the need to come in your "Sunday Best." As you walk in, one of our friendly greeters will give you a paper bulletin with some church information. This is just a helpful guide to what's going on in our church family. We will begin our service with songs, some new, some older, like traditional hymns. After we sing, we have a few minutes where everyone greets each other; we call this "the passing of the peace." Here, we give hugs, handshakes, and high fives, and we try to make everyone feel loved and welcome. You will also hear a a message from our Pastor, Eddie Cuen, who will undoubtedly tie in bacon to the Bible passage he is teaching from. Some of our volunteers will collect an offering, where many of the church members will give a financial gift to the church; this is not something you should feel pressured to do, as this is a way church members give back to their local church. We will sing a song or two after this, then Pastor Eddie will give a blessing, signaling the end of the service. Feel free to stick around and talk to us after the service!

Children's Church

Children will spend the opening songs and greeting time with their families. After this, they will have the option to go to Children's Church with our Family Life Coordinator, Chloe Langford. Parents can take their kids to the back of the church to meet Chloe and others attending Children's Church for that week. She will then take the kids to the 1st grade room on campus and lead them in story time, crafts, and play time, focused around Biblical stories and truths. Kids Kindergarten-5th Grade are encouraged to attend. First time? Another parent or church member would be happy to guide you to the classroom, or you can arrange for Chloe to bring back your child to you at the end of the service.
